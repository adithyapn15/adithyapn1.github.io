Adithya Pradeep

Motto: research on cool stuff.

Doctoral candidate in Engineering Science at the University of Oxford.

I work at the intersection of topology, soft matter, and photonics. I develop semi classical models for liquid crystal textures and test them in the lab. Current projects include metastable topological domains in nematic devices, hybrid strategies for reconfigurable liquid crystal patterning, and speckle based coherence engineering for sub Rayleigh imaging. I like connecting theory, numerical simulation, and experiments.

Currently: DPhil researcher in the Soft Matter Photonics Group at Oxford, working on topological solitons and defects in liquid crystals, hologram microscopy, smart windows, and blue phase structures. Previously: MSc in Applied Physics and Applied Mathematics at Columbia University, on thin film transistors, laser induced phase transitions in display stacks, and racetrack style memory devices. Earlier projects included structural materials, metallic glasses, corrosion, and embedded systems with simple AI and control.
